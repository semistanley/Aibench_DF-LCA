Abstract 3 
With the advancement of digitisation, data is being collected, transmitted and used in a wide 4 
range of domains, and ICT estimates that by 2035, the world will have generated 180 ZB of data, and 5 
that in three hundred years' time, the number of bits generated worldwide may exceed the number of 6 
atoms on the planet, and virtual environments (VE) may become unsustainable. In this context, 7 
Digitization Footprint (DF) has been proposed to support the analysis of VE sustainability by 8 
parameterising the amount of digital information. At present, the lack of methods for DF evaluation 9 
has led to the fact that the current research on DF only stays in the quantification of data volume, and 10 
does not quantify the time, effort (costs invested for data storage, processing or transfer). To address 11 
this problem, this paper proposes a DF -LCA framework based on Data Processing Units (DPUs), 12 

--- PAGE 2 ---
 
 
through which the DF of an information system can be scientifically and completely 13 
quantified.Considering the complexity of the information system's internal situation (data transfer, 14 
module coupling), it is a challenge to monitor the internal data as well as the metrics. Therefore, by 15 
abstracting the information system's data manipulation process into a DPU, which is a logical unit, it 16 
is possible to obtain the information system's DF by monitoring it from the outside without having to 17 
study the information system's complex interior, which greatly simplifies the difficulty of quantifying 18 
the DF. In reality, data may be processed multiple times, and the processing may be distributed over 19 
multiple DPUs, and quantifying the DF of a single DPU does not fully satisfy the needs of DF 20 
assessment. To address this problem, this paper improves the LCA to DF -LCA by defining the 21 
topology between DPUs, clarifying the network relationship of data transmission from DPU to DPU, 22 
and finally obtaining the data link of the output data. Finally the DPUs on the data link of this data 23 
are monitored with indicators, and the DF of this data can be obtained.In order to solve the problem 24 
of transmission error on the link, this paper is based on the existing LCA method, and improves the 25 
data bloodline in the big data, which achieves the function of tracking the data transmission problem 26 
on the link. In addition, this paper optimises the hierarchical structure of LCA to make it more 27 
applicable to the analysis of DPU topology results of information systems, and finally integrates the 28 
three dimensions of performance, energy consumption and value into the index system of DF -LCA. 29 
Through the above methods, this paper establishes a generic DF assessment framework and provides 30 
basic model support for DF standardisation. 31

1 Introduction  34 
In 1946, ENIAC marked a crucial moment in human history as it marked its entrance into digital 35 
technology. Since that time, digital electronic technologies have powered an extraordinary revolution 36 
that is revolutionizing how information is stored, processed and transmitted. Digitization has seen 37 
unprecedented acceleration over the years, creating exponential data generation rates 38 
globally(Tihinen et al., 20 16). Today, digitization is continuing at an unprecedented rate and the 39 
amount of data generated globally is exploding. Information and Communication Technology (ICT) 40 
(Petroc Taylor,.2023)estimates that the world will generate 180 ZB of data by 2025, and assuming a 41 
growth rate of 20 per cent, more bits will be generated than all the atoms of the Earth between now 42 
and 2350 years from now（V opson ,.2022）. 43 
However, there is a growing debate about whether the digital resources needed to store, process, 44 
and transmit this burgeoning volume of data can keep up with its rapid growth. Marinello et al. 45 
addressed this concern by framing it as an issue of virtual environment (VE) sustainability(Marinello 46 
et al., 2016). VE sustainability aims to achieve longer term benefits and impacts in the digitization 47 
process by maintaining VE with as few resources as possible. 48 
To address the issue of VE sustainability, Marinello et al. also proposed the concept of 49 
Digitization Footprint (DF).DF refers to the cost, time, and volume of data generated by the processes 50 
of storing, transferring, and processing data in the VE (Marinello et al., 20 16). Parameterising the 51 
resources consumed by the digitization process through DF can provide data support for VE 52 
sustainability. However, the current challenge is the lack of a framework system to quantify DF. 53
